05 — Ethics

Responsible AI &
System Design

Building QA and conversational systems involves choices that carry real consequences — for what information users receive, what answers are treated as authoritative, and who benefits or is harmed by system failures. This course weaves ethical considerations directly into each week's evaluation and design work.

🎭
Hallucination & Factual Grounding
QA and RAG systems can generate fluent but factually incorrect answers with high confidence. We study RAG as a grounding mechanism, evaluate faithfulness explicitly with RAGAS, and build error classification practices that distinguish retrieval failures from generation failures.
⚠️
Bias in Retrieved & Generated Content
Retrieval systems reflect the biases of their source corpora. Models inherit training data biases that can surface in answers to sensitive questions. Students are expected to include bias analysis in their error classification reports and flag systematic demographic or topical gaps.
🔒
Privacy in Dialogue Systems
Conversational systems often handle sensitive user inputs. We discuss what data should be logged vs. discarded, how dialogue state tracking can expose personal information, and the responsibilities of developers building systems that interact with real users.
🤖
Safety in Multi-Agent Systems
Multi-agent architectures introduce new failure modes: runaway loops, compounding errors, uncontrolled API costs, and emergent behaviour that no single agent was designed to produce. Loop-guarding and cost tracking are not just engineering concerns — they are responsible deployment requirements.
Responsible System Design Principles
✅
Factual Accuracy
Ground answers in verifiable sources; evaluate faithfulness explicitly
🔍
Transparency
Document system architecture, data sources, and known limitations
🛡️
Privacy
Minimise logging of user inputs; handle dialogue data responsibly
🔁
Safety & Control
Implement termination conditions and cost limits in agentic systems
⚖️
Fairness
Identify and report systematic gaps in system performance across topics
Embedded in every report
Error classification · Bias flagging · Architecture documentation · Limitation statements
