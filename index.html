About

Background &
Academic Profile

I am an Assistant Professor in Natural Language Processing, with a strong background in machine learning, multilingual NLP, and AI for education. My work focuses on developing human-centered intelligent systems that address real-world challenges in learning, communication, and language technology. My research particularly emphasizes multilingual representation learning, emotion recognition, and the use of generative AI to support inclusive and personalized educational applications.

I have extensive experience in teaching, curriculum development, and supervising student research across a wide range of subjects, including Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning, Data Science, Language Technology, and Large Language Models. Over the years, I have taught at multiple institutions and have contributed to the design and delivery of courses that integrate theoretical foundations with practical applications.

My broader research vision centers on building scalable, interpretable, and socially meaningful AI systems. I am particularly interested in advancing NLP for low-resource languages, developing emotionally intelligent conversational technologies, and designing AI-driven educational tools that enhance student engagement and learning outcomes. Alongside my teaching and research, I am committed to mentoring students and junior academics, contributing to applied research initiatives, and fostering interdisciplinary collaboration.

BSc AI · University of Groningen
Language Technology Practicals

A hands-on practical course for BSc Artificial Intelligence students covering the full NLP pipeline: from text representation and vector semantics to RNNs, transformers, dialogue systems, and RAG-based QA systems. Structured around PBL projects with milestone-based timelines and peer code review.

Advanced · University of Groningen
Large Language Models & Applications

Deep dive into transformer architectures, instruction fine-tuning, RLHF, retrieval-augmented generation, and multi-agent systems. Covers both the theoretical foundations of modern LLMs and their practical deployment in real-world NLP applications. Lecture materials available.

NLP Fundamentals: Vectors, RNNs & Transformers

Lecture modules covering vector semantics and embeddings, recurrent neural networks (sequence modelling, LSTMs), and the transformer architecture. Developed for the Language Technology Practicals series with custom slide decks and coding notebooks.
